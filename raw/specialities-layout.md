## Tier Definitions

| Tier | Who is reading? | What are they deciding? | Emotional register | Target words |
|------|----------------|------------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Tier 1** | Caregiver / family member | "How do we manage this life change?" | Meet them in grief, fear, or exhaustion | 2,800–3,500 |
| **Tier 2** | Patient (self-navigating) | "Do I need a specialist, and is this the right one?" | Meet them in frustration or anxiety | 2,200–2,800 |
| **Tier 3** | Patient (self-navigating) | "Is this neurological or orthopaedic? Do I need surgery?" | Meet them in practical problem-solving | 1,600–2,200 |

## Page Architecture — Hub vs. Single Page

Some conditions have distinct sub-types that each need clinical depth. These use a **hub + spoke** model: one main page covering the condition as a whole, with optional detail pages for specific sub-types.

| Condition | Page model | Hub covers | Spokes (detail pages, if needed) |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------------------------------|
| Stroke & Brain Attack | Hub + spokes | Unified "Could this be you?", red flags, caregiver, FAQ | Ischaemic Stroke · Haemorrhagic Stroke · TIA · Stroke Rehab |
| Dementia & Alzheimer's | Hub + spokes | Unified early signs, caregiver, daily tips, FAQ | Alzheimer's · Vascular Dementia · FTD · MCI |
| Parkinsonism | Hub + spokes | Unified overview, caregiver, FAQ | Parkinson's Disease · Essential Tremor · Dystonia · Ataxia |
| Epilepsy & Seizures | Hub + spokes | Unified overview, first aid, stigma, FAQ | Focal · Generalised · Status Epilepticus · Febrile Seizures |
| Headache Disorders | Single page | Migraine + Tension + Cluster in one page | Vertigo has its own separate page |
| Vertigo | Single page | BPPV + Vestibular Neuritis + Ménière's + Central | — |
| Neuropathy | Single page | Diabetic + Peripheral + Hereditary + Toxic | — |
| Neuro Infections | Single page | Meningitis + Encephalitis + NCC + TB Meningitis | — |
| Neuro Rehabilitation | Single page | Post-Stroke + TBI + Spasticity + Gait | — |
| Back Pain | Single page | Disc disease + Radiculopathy + Sciatica + Stenosis | — |
| Cervical & Neck Pain | Single page | Spondylosis + Myelopathy + Radiculopathy | — |

**Hub page rule:** The hub must be a complete, self-contained page — not a table of contents. It carries the full emotional architecture (hero, caregiver, FAQ, myths, daily tips). Spokes are optional deep-dives for patients who already know their specific diagnosis.

## Special Rules

### Emergency Banner (Stroke, Neuro Infections only)

Visually distinct from the standard red-flags section. Appears at the top of the condition block, above "Could this be you?"

**Copy (Stroke):**
> ⚠️ Stroke is a medical emergency. If you or someone near you has sudden face drooping, arm weakness, or speech difficulty — call an ambulance or go to the nearest emergency room immediately. Do not wait. Do not book a clinic appointment. If you are near Kanpur, go to Paras Health — Dr. Kumar coordinates stroke care there.

**Copy (Neuro Infections):**
> ⚠️ If you or someone near you has high fever with severe headache and stiff neck, confusion, or seizures — this may be a brain infection. Go to the nearest emergency room immediately. Do not wait for an outpatient appointment.

### Stigma Callout (Epilepsy, Dementia, Parkinson's)

A distinct visual block — not buried in prose. Placed after "Your first consultation."

**Copy (Epilepsy):**
> Epilepsy is not a mental illness. It is not contagious. It is not caused by supernatural forces or past sins. It is a medical condition of the brain — and in most cases, it is treatable. If anyone has told you otherwise, they were wrong. You deserve proper medical care without shame.

**Copy (Dementia):**
> Memory loss in a parent or grandparent is not "just old age." It is not laziness, stubbornness, or something they can control. Dementia is a medical condition that affects the brain, and early diagnosis can genuinely help. There is no shame in seeking help — it is the most loving thing a family can do.

**Copy (Parkinson's):**
> A tremor does not mean your life is over. Parkinson's is a treatable condition — most patients continue to live active, independent lives for many years with the right medical support. If you've been avoiding a diagnosis out of fear, know this: the earlier treatment begins, the better the outcomes.

### Hindi-First Content Flags

These sections should be written in conversational Hindi first, then translated to English — because they address beliefs and stigmas most commonly expressed in Hindi-speaking households:

| Section | Conditions |
|---------|-----------|
| Myths & Facts (all pairs) | Epilepsy, Dementia |
| Stigma callout | Epilepsy, Dementia, Parkinson's |
| Caregiver section | Dementia, Stroke |
| Red flags / Emergency banner | Stroke, Neuro Infections |
| Myths about painkillers, eye doctor, stress | Headache Disorders |

### Neuro vs. Ortho Distinction (Back Pain, Cervical Pain only)

Tier 3 pages must answer the question patients are actually asking: "Should I be seeing a neurologist or an orthopaedic surgeon?"

Add a brief callout in the overview or "What's happening inside" section:

> **When is back pain a neurology problem?** Most back pain is muscular or skeletal — and an orthopaedic surgeon is the right doctor. But if you have numbness, tingling, weakness in the legs, or changes in bladder/bowel control, the problem may involve the nerves or spinal cord. That's when a neurologist needs to evaluate you. Dr. Kumar works alongside orthopaedic colleagues to make sure you're with the right specialist.

### "Your First Consultation" — Shared vs. Custom

The core structure is the same across all pages (conversation → examination → investigations → plan → what to bring → fee). Customise only the examination paragraph per condition:

| Condition | Examination detail to mention |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| Headache | Neurological exam; Dix-Hallpike for vertigo |
| Epilepsy | Neurological exam; EEG may be scheduled |
| Stroke | Neurological exam; review of CT/MRI from hospital |
| Parkinson's | Movement assessment; gait and tremor evaluation |
| Dementia | Cognitive screening (MMSE/MoCA); detailed history from family |
| Neuropathy | Nerve conduction study (NCS) may be scheduled |
| Back/Cervical | Reflex and sensation testing; review of MRI spine |

## SEO Architecture

### Expanded TOC with Entity Tags

The "On this page" table of contents serves a dual purpose: patient navigation AND clinical entity surfacing for search engines. Each TOC entry includes the main section title as the anchor link, plus sub-condition entity tags displayed as secondary labels.

**Format:**

```
[Section Title] → #anchor
  Entity Tag · Entity Tag · Entity Tag · Entity Tag
```

**Entity tags per condition:**

| Condition | TOC entry | Entity tags |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Headache Disorders | Headache & Migraine | Migraine · Tension headache · Cluster headache · Medication-overuse headache |
| Headache Disorders | Vertigo & Dizziness | BPPV · Vestibular neuritis · Ménière's disease · Central vertigo |
| Epilepsy & Seizures | Epilepsy & Seizures | Focal epilepsy · Generalised epilepsy · Status epilepticus · Febrile seizures |
| Stroke & Brain Attack | Stroke & Brain Attack | Ischaemic stroke · Haemorrhagic stroke · TIA (mini-stroke) · Stroke rehabilitation |
| Parkinsonism | Parkinsonism & Movement | Parkinson's disease · Essential tremor · Dystonia · Ataxia · Atypical parkinsonism |
| Dementia & Alzheimer's | Dementia & Alzheimer's | Alzheimer's disease · Vascular dementia · Frontotemporal dementia · Mild cognitive impairment |
| Vertigo | Vertigo & Dizziness | BPPV · Vestibular neuritis · Ménière's disease · Central vertigo · Labyrinthitis |
| Neuropathy | Neuropathy | Diabetic neuropathy · Peripheral neuropathy · Hereditary neuropathy · Toxic neuropathy |
| Neuro Infections | Neuro Infections | Bacterial meningitis · Viral encephalitis · Neurocysticercosis · Tubercular meningitis |
| Back Pain | Back Pain | Lumbar disc disease · Radiculopathy · Sciatica · Spinal stenosis |
| Cervical & Neck Pain | Cervical & Neck Pain | Cervical spondylosis · Cervical myelopathy · Cervical radiculopathy |
| Neuro Rehabilitation | Neuro Rehabilitation | Post-stroke rehab · TBI recovery · Spasticity management · Gait disorders |

**Design note:** Entity tags should be styled as small, muted pill-shaped labels beneath each TOC anchor — visually secondary to the section title. Use the same pill/tag component already used on the homepage specialty cards. They are clickable only if the sub-condition has its own anchor or spoke page; otherwise they are static text.

**For single-section pages (Tier 3):** There is no multi-entry TOC, but the entity tags still appear as a row of pills directly beneath the "Why this matters" overview paragraph. Same visual treatment, same SEO benefit.

### Schema Markup (JSON-LD)

Every specialty page must include structured data in the page `<head>` using JSON-LD. This is invisible to patients but feeds Google's health panels, knowledge graph, and rich snippets directly.

**Required schema types per page:**

**1. MedicalWebPage** (one per page)
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "MedicalWebPage",
  "name": "Headache Disorders — Dr. Pradeep Kumar Neurology",
  "description": "Expert diagnosis and treatment for migraine, tension headache, cluster headache, and medication-overuse headache at Paras Health Kanpur and Equinox Neuro Clinic Lucknow.",
  "url": "https://drpradeepkumar.example/en/specialties/headache-disorders",
  "author": {
    "@type": "Physician",
    "name": "Dr. Pradeep Kumar",
    "medicalSpecialty": "Neurology",
    "qualifications": "MBBS, MD (Medicine), DM (Neurology)"
  },
  "lastReviewed": "2026-05-31"
}
```

**2. MedicalCondition** (one per sub-condition on the page)
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "MedicalCondition",
  "name": "Migraine",
  "alternateName": ["Migraine headache", "Migraine disorder"],
  "associatedAnatomy": {
    "@type": "AnatomicalStructure",
    "name": "Brain"
  },
  "signOrSymptom": [
    {"@type": "MedicalSymptom", "name": "Throbbing unilateral headache"},
    {"@type": "MedicalSymptom", "name": "Nausea"},
    {"@type": "MedicalSymptom", "name": "Photophobia"},
    {"@type": "MedicalSymptom", "name": "Phonophobia"}
  ],
  "possibleTreatment": [
    {"@type": "MedicalTherapy", "name": "CGRP inhibitor therapy"},
    {"@type": "MedicalTherapy", "name": "Botox injections"},
    {"@type": "MedicalTherapy", "name": "Preventive medication"}
  ]
}
```

**3. FAQPage** (one per page, wrapping all FAQ questions)
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "Can a headache be a sign of something serious?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "Usually not — over 95% of headaches are primary..."
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

**4. Physician** (shared across all pages, can be defined once and referenced)
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Physician",
  "name": "Dr. Pradeep Kumar",
  "medicalSpecialty": "Neurology",
  "qualifications": "MBBS, MD (Medicine), DM (Neurology)",
  "worksFor": [
    {"@type": "Hospital", "name": "Paras Health Kanpur"},
    {"@type": "MedicalClinic", "name": "Equinox Neuro Clinic, Lucknow"}
  ],
  "address": [
    {"@type": "PostalAddress", "addressLocality": "Kanpur", "addressRegion": "Uttar Pradesh"},
    {"@type": "PostalAddress", "addressLocality": "Lucknow", "addressRegion": "Uttar Pradesh"}
  ]
}
```

**Implementation notes:**
- Combine all schema objects into a single `<script type="application/ld+json">` block with an `@graph` array
- The `MedicalCondition` entries should list every sub-condition shown in the entity tags — this is where the SEO entity matching happens
- Include `alternateName` for conditions with common Hindi or colloquial names (e.g., "lakwa" for paralysis/stroke, "mirgi" for epilepsy) — Google indexes these for Hindi-language searches
- Update `lastReviewed` date whenever page content is medically reviewed
- The `FAQPage` schema enables rich snippet accordion in Google SERPs — high-impact for click-through rate
